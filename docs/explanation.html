APY vs APR in Crypto

Understanding how compounding affects your digital asset returns.

What is APR?

Annual Percentage Rate (APR) represents the simple annual interest rate without any compounding. In DeFi lending platforms like Compound or Aave, the base borrow and lend rates are usually quoted in APR.

Simple Interest

What is APY?

Annual Percentage Yield (APY) is the effective annual rate that accounts for compounding interest. In staking (e.g., Polkadot or Cardano), rewards often compound daily, making the APY significantly higher than the APR.

Compounded Interest

Why it Matters in DeFi

  • Yield Farming: Compounding exponentially boosts returns in liquidity pools like Uniswap.
  • Maximizing Gains: Daily compounding (common in crypto) maximizes growth compared to simple APR.
  • The Gap: A 10% APR compounded daily results in ~10.47% APY—that extra 0.47% adds up in large portfolios.
"The difference between APR and APY is the difference between earning interest on your principal versus earning interest on your interest."
